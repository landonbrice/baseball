# Velocity Layer Framework — Landon Brice (Year-1 Conservative Build)

Synthesizes the **Velocity / CNS** and **Layer 3 — Loading** rows from
`drill_library_deduped.csv` (and the combined raw CSV) into a velocity-development framework
that respects Landon's **flexor-pronator / medial-elbow history**.

**The governing constraint (from `00_Pitcher_Dossier_LandonBrice.md`):** this is a conservative
build year. **No 7oz+ overload. No run-and-throw pulldowns. No sustained max-intent** until
Phase 6 at the earliest. **Forearm tightness is the stop sign**; UCL-area sensation on elbow
extension = back off. Underload (3-4oz) for CNS/arm-speed is preferred. Everything below is
filtered through that lens — the agents flagged the aggressive content as "year-2 / not-now"
and it is deferred, not deleted (see §5).

> **Why velocity is downstream of mechanics here:** Driveline's force-plate research
> (*Analyzing Front and Back Leg Ground Forces*) and the IJSPT kinematics study
> (*Range of Motion, Hip-Shoulder Separation & Kinematics*, PMC7727427) both show velocity is
> predicted by **stride-leg ground force** and **hip-shoulder separation timing**, not by
> throwing harder. For Landon, the highest-leverage "velocity" work is the lead-leg-block and
> separation programs in `cue_programs.md`. This framework is the *arm-speed and CNS layer that
> sits underneath that*, kept deliberately low-stress.

## 1. Weighted-Ball Protocol (≤6oz, sub-max) — what IS allowed

**Allowed this year:**

- **Plyo-ball constraint work, ≤6oz / ≤~225g effective, sub-max intent.** The daily driver is
  the **Plyo Ball Routine — Warmup Progression** (Driveline). The full three-round routine
  *includes* heavy 1-2kg plyos, but they are used at **25-50% intent for pattern, never as
  max-intent overload** — that is the only way the heavy balls enter the program. Lighter
  rounds (100-225g, ~3.5-8oz soft plyo) run at 50-75%.
  - *Source:* Driveline Blog, Plyo Ball Routine
    (https://www.drivelinebaseball.com/2017/12/plyo-velocity-weighted-balls-replication/),
    `density_score 5`.
- **The plyocare drill set as pattern + arm-care**, not as a velocity stimulus:
  Reverse Throws → Pivot Pickoffs → Roll-Ins → Rockers → Drop Steps → Walking Windups, all in
  the ≤450g (soft) range, sub-max. These are tagged Layer 2-3 and double as the mechanics
  drills in `cue_programs.md`.
- **Bauer's Daily Weighted Ball / Plyo Routine** (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VIDd2yMSSnY)
  — *framed conservatively, weights light*. Use the structure (pivot pickoffs, reverse throws)
  not the heavy-ball intent.
- **Hybrid B plyo day (~70% intent)** is acceptable mid-progression (Phase 5) once a base is
  built — graded force onto the staying-closed/rotation patterns. It is the **ceiling** for the
  build year.

**Explicitly NOT allowed this year:**

- **7oz+ overload balls.** *Comparing Overload vs. Underload Weighted Ball Stress* (Driveline
  research) and the *Weighted-Implement 6-Week Program Study* (PeerJ, PMC6254244) document the
  overload protocols — both are tagged **"year-2 / not-now."** Driveline themselves rarely
  exceed 9oz; we cap well below that.
- **Hybrid A plyo day (~90% intent)** — tagged year-2 / not-now; defer sustained 90% to Phase 6+.
- **Pulldowns / run-and-throw max-effort throws** — deferred to Phase 6 minimum (see §4).

**Practical weekly shape (Phase 3-5):** plyo warmup routine every throwing day (sub-max) +
2 throwing days where the plyocare drills carry the mechanics cue + 1 recovery-day light-plyo
session. No standalone "velocity day."

## 2. CNS-Firing Protocol

The goal is to train **arm speed and rate of force development without medial-elbow load**.
The library's CNS-safe tools:

- **Rotational medicine-ball throws** — *Are Rotational Medicine Ball Throws the Key to
  Pitching Velocity?* (Tread, https://treadathletics.com/rmbt/). Scoop / shotput / side throws,
  3 x 5/side. Transverse-plane power that correlates with throwing velocity and is
  **arm-stress-free** — ideal CNS driver for Landon. **Primary CNS tool.**
- **3 Drills To Throw Harder By Transferring Energy** (Tread,
  https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rsPBZZly-6M) — ground-force → hip → braced front leg → trunk
  → arm, sub-max, ≤6oz. Trains the *sequence* the velocity comes from.
- **Reverse Throws (plyo)** — deceleration / CNS staple (Driveline + Bauer, sources_count 2),
  underload-friendly, ≤6oz/450g soft. Trains the eccentric/decel side of arm speed.
- **Velocity-Based Training for Baseball Athletes** (Driveline,
  https://www.drivelinebaseball.com/2017/04/velocity-based-training-for-baseball-athletes/) —
  **high-leverage for Landon specifically** because he's an advanced lifter (trap-bar DL 350,
  front squat 250). Bar-speed-autoregulated power work in the weight room builds the CNS/power
  base for arm speed with **zero throwing-side elbow stress**. Lean into this.

**Frequency:** med-ball + VBT power work 2x/week, separated from high-volume throwing days.
Rotational med-ball throws can pair with the Hip Rotation program as the "power expression" of
the same pattern.

## 3. Underweight-Ball (3-4oz) Recommendations

This is the **preferred** way to train arm speed for Landon — underload moves the arm *faster*
with *less* force than a 5oz ball, which is exactly the trade-off a flexor-pronator history
wants.

- **Evidence:** *Comparing Overload vs. Underload Weighted Ball Stress* (Driveline research,
  https://www.drivelinebaseball.com/2017/03/comparing-overload-vs-underload-weighted-ball-stress-on-the-arm/)
  — "insufficient evidence that under- or over-load is more stressful than a 5oz ball at max
  effort; underload moves the arm faster with less force." Driveline never goes below 3oz.
- **Application:** use **3-4oz** balls for short arm-speed sets at *controlled* (not max)
  intent — e.g. light catch-play arm-speed reps, or the lightest round of the plyo routine
  swapped for a 3-4oz ball. The lightest 100g plyo (~3.5oz) in the Driveline warmup routine
  already lives in this range.
- **The supporting study** (*Weighted-Implement 6-Week Program*, PeerJ) found
  **internal-rotation velocity rose significantly** with weighted-implement work even when
  group velocity didn't — i.e. the arm-speed adaptation is real and underload is the safe lane
  to chase it. We take the *underload* lesson and leave the *max-intent / 2-7oz* protocol for
  year 2.
- **Boundary:** stay **at or above 3oz** (never below) and keep intent sub-max. If forearm
  tightness shows up, the 3-4oz work is the first thing to cut.

## 4. Intent-Training Notes (delayed to Phase 6 minimum)

Intent — true max-effort throwing, pulldowns, velocity testing — is **deferred to Phase 6 at
the earliest**, and only after the lead-leg block and separation hold in a bullpen.

- **Catch-play with intent, restrained:** *Optimizing Catch-Play — Tips with Trev Ep 10*
  (Bauer) reframes catch-play as intentional mechanics reps **without** max effort. This is the
  *only* "intent" allowed through Phase 5 — intent of *purpose*, not of *effort*.
- **Long toss:** *Long Toss Mechanics* (Bauer/SI) builds arm strength + connection, but the
  **aggressive pull-down arc is explicitly deferred to Phase 6** per the conservative-load
  filter. Stretch out the throw for connection; do not crow-hop and pull down at max.
- **Hybrid A (~90% intent) plyo days** and **sustained max-intent ladders**: Phase 6+ only,
  gated on a clean elbow.
- **Gate to unlock intent (Phase 6):** (a) lead-leg block holds in a 75% bullpen,
  (b) hip-shoulder separation visible on 240fps film, (c) **no forearm tightness** across a
  full throwing week, (d) workload/torque trending flat (see monitoring below). Miss any one →
  stay in Phase 5.

**Monitoring discipline (runs the whole build):** *Pitching Velocity & UCL Stress (PULSE)* and
*Workload, Range of Motion, and Early Season Injuries* (both Driveline research) make the case
for tracking elbow torque and ROM as velocity rises. *13 Tips for Mighty Elbows and Wrists*,
*Banded Pronation*, *Banded Ulnar Deviation (FCU)*, and *Palmar Fascia Soft-Tissue Work*
(Cressey) are the **non-negotiable durability block** — run them year-round; they are what
makes any of the above safe for Landon's medial elbow.

### One-paragraph summary for the coach

Landon's velocity will come from the **lead-leg block and hip-shoulder separation** (see
`cue_programs.md`), not from throwing harder — the force-plate and kinematics research is
unambiguous on that. The arm-speed/CNS layer underneath is built **conservatively**: daily
sub-max plyo pattern work (≤6oz), rotational med-ball + velocity-based weight-room power (where
his advanced lifting is a real edge), and short **3-4oz underload** arm-speed sets — with a
year-round flexor-pronator durability block as the safety net. **Intent, overload, and
pulldowns are deferred to Phase 6 minimum, gated on a clean elbow and a block that holds in a
bullpen.** Forearm tightness ends the session, every time.
