# 04 — Splits and Schedules

## A) In-season starter (7-day rotation) — 2 lift model

### Weekly skeleton
- **Start Day (D0):** pitch
- **D+1:** Lift A (highest training stress of week)
- **D+2:** Recovery + light power/med-ball (optional)
- **D+3:** Lift B (moderate)
- **D+4:** Mobility + cuff/scap + low CNS
- **D+5:** Short primer (jumps/throws/light lift)
- **D+6:** Pre-start prep only

### Lift A (D+1)
- Trap bar deadlift or squat: 3–5 x 3–5
- Unilateral lower (split squat): 2–3 x 5–8
- Row variation: 3 x 6–10
- Bench or landmine press: 2–3 x 4–8
- Cuff/scap circuit: 2 rounds

### Lift B (D+3)
- Front squat or RDL: 3–4 x 3–6
- Jump variation: 3–5 x 2–3
- Pull-up/chin-up: 3 x 4–8
- Anti-rotation + med-ball rotational throw: 2–4 sets
- Forearm/grip work: 2 sets

---

## B) In-season starter (6-day rotation) — 2 concise lift model
- **D0:** pitch
- **D+1:** Lift A (brief heavy)
- **D+2:** Recovery/arm care
- **D+3:** Lift B (power + maintenance)
- **D+4:** Mobility, walk-through prep
- **D+5:** primer / bullpen integration

Keep total weekly hard sets slightly lower than 7-day model.

---

## C) In-season reliever — flexible micro-dose model

### If used in game (high stress)
- Next day: tissue recovery, cuff/scap, lower-volume maintenance only

### If not used for 2–3 days
- Add concise whole-body lift:
  - Main lower: 2–3 x 3–5
  - Main upper pull/push: 2–3 x 4–8 each
  - Jump/med-ball: 2–4 sets

### Weekly target
- 2–3 brief lifts (20–45 min), auto-regulated by appearance pattern.

---

## D) Off-season split options

### Option 1: 4-day upper/lower performance split
- Mon: Lower strength + jumps
- Tue: Upper strength + med-ball
- Thu: Lower power + unilateral emphasis
- Fri: Upper power + cuff/scap/forearm density

### Option 2: 3-day total-body split
- Mon: Heavy lower + upper pull
- Wed: Power + upper push/pull + trunk rotation
- Fri: Heavy hinge + mixed upper + med-ball

### Option 3: High/Low model (throwing integrated)
- High CNS days: mound/high-intent throw + strength/power
- Low days: recovery, mobility, skill/feel work, easy conditioning

---

## Progression rules (all schedules)
1. Add load only if bar speed/intent and recovery stay high.
2. If mound stress spikes, reduce gym volume first.
3. Keep at least one high-quality strength exposure weekly in-season when possible.
4. Never chase failure in-season.
