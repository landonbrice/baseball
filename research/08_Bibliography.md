# 08 — Bibliography (with Evidence-Quality Notes)

## Peer-reviewed / scientific

1. Escamilla RF, et al. (2012). Comparison of three baseball-specific 6-week training programs on throwing velocity in high school baseball players. JSCR.  
   Link: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22549085/>  
   Quality note: Controlled intervention; useful for applied programming, limited to high-school context.

2. Carter AB, et al. (2007). Effects of high volume upper extremity plyometric training on throwing velocity. J Strength Cond Res.  
   Link: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17313281/>  
   Quality note: Controlled collegiate study, older and small sample.

3. Szymanski DJ, et al. (2010). Effects of a 4-week youth baseball conditioning program on throwing velocity. JSCR.  
   Link: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21068687/>  
   Quality note: Randomized youth intervention; short duration.

4. Tyler TF, et al. (2010). Preseason shoulder strength measurements in professional baseball pitchers. Am J Sports Med.  
   Link: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20489215/>  
   Quality note: Prospective cohort in pro pitchers; strong applied injury-risk relevance.

5. Fleisig GS, et al. (2017). Biomechanical Analysis of Weighted-Ball Exercises for Baseball Pitchers. Sports Health.  
   Link: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27872403/>  
   Quality note: Controlled biomechanics; mechanism-rich, not long-term injury outcome.

6. Melugin HP, et al. (2021). The Evidence Behind Weighted Ball Throwing Programs for the Baseball Player. Curr Rev Musculoskelet Med.  
   Link: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33403626/>  
   Quality note: Narrative review; broad synthesis, susceptible to source heterogeneity.

7. O’Connell ME, et al. (2022). Weighted Baseball Training Affects Arm Speed Without Increasing Elbow and Shoulder Joint Kinetics. J Appl Biomech.  
   Link: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35981710/>  
   Quality note: Acute lab study; strong mechanistic insight, limited external validity.

8. Pozzi F, et al. (2020). Incidence and risk factors for musculoskeletal disorders of the elbow in baseball pitchers: systematic review.  
   Link: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33487638/>  
   Quality note: Systematic review; evidence quality depends on included study quality.

9. Bullock GS, et al. (2020). Injuries and associated risk factors of the shoulder and elbow among adolescent baseball pitchers: systematic review/meta-analysis.  
   Link: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32143085/>  
   Quality note: Higher-level evidence; adolescent-specific.

10. Mine K, et al. (2020). Preseason shoulder ROM screening and in-season injury risk in overhead athletes: systematic review/meta-analysis.  
    Link: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31937577/>  
    Quality note: Good synthesis; overhead-athlete generalization beyond baseball needed.

11. Chalmers PN, et al. (2019). Risk Factors for Baseball-Related Arm Injuries: A Systematic Review.  
    Link: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30828579/>  
    Quality note: Prospective-focus strengthens causal interpretation, still observational overall.

12. Resistance-based training programs and baseball throwing outcomes (2023 systematic review).  
    Link: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38125451/>  
    Quality note: Useful synthesis, protocol heterogeneity limits precise prescriptions.

13. Specific strength training and throwing velocity in overarm sports (2024 systematic review).  
    Link: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39520628/>  
    Quality note: Cross-sport review; external validity to baseball requires interpretation.

14. Rotator cuff training with upper-extremity BFR in collegiate pitchers (2023 randomized trial).  
    Link: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36933646/>  
    Quality note: RCT-level evidence, but narrow context and sample.

15. In-season strength-power training in team sports (contextual evidence).  
    Link: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31805772/>  
    Quality note: Non-baseball transfer evidence, still useful for microcycle logic.

16. Concurrent strength-endurance youth meta-analysis.  
    Link: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30131714/>  
    Quality note: Meta-analytic, but not baseball-specific.

17. Scheduling training/recovery in-season weekly microcycle (team sport practitioner insights).  
    Link: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30922202/>  
    Quality note: Applied review; indirect evidence for baseball periodization decisions.

## Applied / practice-facing

18. Driveline Baseball (2017). Baseball In-Season Lifting Program: Managing Volume, Intensity, and Fatigue.  
    Link: <https://www.drivelinebaseball.com/2017/03/season-lifting-managing-volume-intensity-fatigue/>  
    Quality note: Coaching methodology article; non-peer-reviewed but practically detailed.

19. Driveline Baseball (2016). Building an In-Season Training Routine for Starting Pitchers – Part I.  
    Link: <https://www.drivelinebaseball.com/2016/09/building-season-training-routine-starting-pitchers-part/>  
    Quality note: Applied periodization framework.

20. Driveline Baseball (2021). Case Study: The Importance of In-Season Training.  
    Link: <https://www.drivelinebaseball.com/2021/08/case-study-the-importance-of-in-season-training/>  
    Quality note: Case study/anecdotal; useful for illustrating detraining dynamics.

21. Driveline search index for in-season strength resources.  
    Link: <https://www.drivelinebaseball.com/?s=in-season+strength>  
    Quality note: Index utility source.

22. Tread Athletics (2025). Beware of Program Hopping.  
    Link: <https://treadathletics.com/beware-of-program-hopping/>  
    Quality note: Coaching philosophy/opinion; useful behavior and adherence insights.

23. Tread site search index.  
    Link: <https://treadathletics.com/?s=in-season+training>  
    Quality note: Index utility source.

24. MLB Pitch Smart pitching guidelines.  
    Link: <https://www.mlb.com/pitch-smart/pitching-guidelines>  
    Quality note: Consensus/public health guidance with workload focus.
