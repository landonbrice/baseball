# Injury History

## Primary Injury: Forearm / UCL Area (2024)

**Description:** Significant tightening of the forearm and UCL area that prevented throwing. Not a UCL tear — more of a soft tissue / flexor-pronator complex issue with medial elbow involvement.

**Symptoms:**
- Forearm tightness that radiates to the UCL area
- When arm is sore, extending/straightening the elbow produces a tightening sensation in the UCL area
- Tightness can become severe enough to shut down throwing entirely

**Treatment that worked:**
- Dry needling of the forearm / flexor-pronator mass
- Mechanical soft tissue work
- Pronator strengthening (this was a key piece)
- Gradual return to throwing

## Ongoing / Chronic Patterns

- Forearm tightness is the primary warning sign — treat it as a red flag before it escalates
- UCL area sensation on elbow extension = arm is under stress, reduce intensity
- Pronator weakness appears to be a root cause contributor — maintenance work is non-negotiable
- Uses Rocksauce pre-throwing to activate/warm up the forearm area

## Red Flags (escalate protocol immediately)

- Any tightening sensation in UCL area on elbow extension
- Forearm feels "locked" or difficult to pronate through full range
- Medial elbow soreness after throwing

## Notes for Protocol Generation

- Always include pronator-specific work in recovery protocols
- Nerve glides are important given the medial elbow pattern (ulnar nerve involvement possible)
- Soft tissue work should precede all arm care sessions
- Do NOT rush return to high-intent throwing after any forearm tightness episode
