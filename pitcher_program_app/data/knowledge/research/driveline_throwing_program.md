## Core Principles

### Stress & Supercompensation
- Stressed tissue, given sufficient recovery time, adds capacity (supercompensation)
- For untrained/unrecovered athletes, stress leads to poor performance and injury risk
- If a stressor stays the same week after week, adaptation stops — must progressively vary

### Recovery Importance
- 55-85% of training time should be warmup and recovery
- REST DAYS ARE REST DAYS — don't throw
- Adding more work doesn't make a good program better
- Recovery days: PULSE arm speed must be significantly lower than velocity days

### Common Mistakes
1. Doing too much too fast — throwing program compliance is critical
2. Skipping the on-ramp or combining with other velocity programs
3. Doing offseason throwing programming when in-season
4. Throwing to warm up instead of warming up to throw (Warm Up → Train → Recovery)

## Throwing Warm-Up (Every Throwing Day)

### J-Band Routine — 10 reps each:
- Forward Fly
- Reverse Fly
- Internal Rotation
- External Rotation
- Bicep Curls
- Tricep Extensions

### Wrist Weights — 10 reps each:
- Pronation Swings
- Two-Arm Throws
- Pivot Pickoffs
- Cuban Press
- Note: Less than 10 reps OK when starting out — prioritize technique over volume

## Plyo Ball Drills (with Purpose)

### Drill Order (always in this sequence):
1. **Reverse Throws** (always first)
   - WHY: Trains posterior shoulder to accept force from higher velocities + T-spine mobility
   - HOW: Kneel on throwing-side knee, torso stacked, drive shoulder and elbow back through natural arm slot

2. **Pivot Picks** (always after reverse throws)
   - WHY: More efficient arm action and improved forward rotation
   - HOW: Chest facing target, feet staggered with throwing-arm foot forward, counter rotate and throw

3. **Roll-Ins**
   - WHY: Feel more hip-shoulder separation (lower half stays open, upper half closes)
   - HOW: Face target, walking step with throwing-side leg, drive into foot strike, minimize hip rotation, rotate around braced front leg

4. **Step Backs**
   - WHY: Delay torso and gain better momentum toward the plate
   - HOW: From stretch position, step back ~1 foot, go into leg lift, gain momentum back toward target

5. **Drop Steps**
   - WHY: Gain momentum down the mound and delay torso into foot plant
   - HOW: Back facing target, step back with plant foot (gain ground), move fast down mound, delay torso

6. **Janitors**
   - WHY: Constrained drop step — lower half rotates freely while upper half stays quiet
   - HOW: On rubber facing away from target, slight leg bend, small leg lift, focus on lower-half-first rotation

7. **Rockers**
   - WHY: Proper weight shift and timing of lead leg block
   - HOW: Back foot on rubber, front foot partway down mound, sumo stance, rock side to side, throw on back-foot shift

8. **Walking Windups** (always last)
   - WHY: Put everything together and gain momentum
   - HOW: Face target, step forward, rotate foot as if using rubber, leg lift, stride and throw

### Plyo Routine by Day Type:
| Day Type | Effort Level | # Drills | Peak Arm Speed |
|----------|-------------|----------|----------------|
| Recovery | 50-60% | 2-3 | 500-600 |
| Hybrid B | 60-70% | 5-6 | 700-850 |
| Hybrid A | 80-90% | 5-6 | (not specified) |
| Velocity | 100% | 5-6 | 950-1200+ |

### Plyo Velocity Day Specifics:
- Roll-ins: 2-3 throws with green and blue (or blue and red)
- Step backs/rockers: 2-3 throws with blue and red
- Drop steps/janitors/walking windups: 2-3 throws with blue, red, yellow, gray
- Perform all throws with each color at once

## Throwing Day Types

### Recovery
- Lightest throwing day
- Plyo: reverse throws + pivot picks only (pink and green)
- After plyos: light catch play only
- No extension or compression throws
- Day off from weight room

### Recovery + Short Box
- Same as recovery, but after catch play throw a light short box bullpen (45-50 feet)
- Useful day before a start for guys who like mound reps without big workload

### No Throw Recovery
- NO THROWS — just warm-up routine (stretching, bands, wrist weights, shoulder tube)
- Then post-throw recovery routine
- Best used day after starts or when arm/body needs extra recovery

### Hybrid B (60-70% intent)
- Full plyo routine (reverse throws, pivot picks, 2 prescribed drills, walking windups)
- Long toss: extension throws only (high arc, increasing distance)
- No compression throws, no pulldowns
- Work out to distance, then work back in lightly

### Hybrid A (80-90% intent)
- Same as Hybrid B but higher intent
- After extension throws, work back in for compression throws (8-12 throws, 90-120ft, on-a-line)

### Long Toss Program
- Extension throws with weighted baseballs: 5 throws 11oz (60-75ft), 5 throws 9oz (75-100ft), 5 throws 7oz (100-120ft), 5oz to tolerance
- Compression throws: hard, on-a-line, 90-120ft, ~head high, shuffle approach

### Plyo Velo (100% intent)
- 3 drills: 2 prescribed + walking windups at max effort

### WB Mound Velo
- Weighted ball series: 3oz, 4oz, 5oz (regular), 6oz, 7oz
- 3-4 throws per ball weight
- Short form (beginners): 5oz → 6oz → 4oz
- Long form: 5oz → 6oz → 7oz → 4oz → 3oz

### Mound Velo
- Max effort throws off mound with regular 5oz baseball
- 10-15 throws (3 sets x 5, ~1 min rest between sets)

## Phase Structure (16 Weeks)

### On-Ramp Phase (Weeks 1-4)
- Purpose: Build physical base for velocity phase
- Week 1: All Hybrid B days
- Weeks 2-4: Gradually introduce Hybrid A days
- Pattern: Recovery / Throw / Recovery / Throw / Recovery / Throw / OFF

### Velocity Phase (Weeks 5-10)
- Max 2 velocity days per week
- Can drop to 1 velo day if athlete is fatigued
- Week 10 is a deload (all Hybrid B)
- Progression: Hybrid A → Plyo Velo → WB Mound Velo → Mound Velo

### Competition Phase (Weeks 11-14)
- Pitch design (baseline → shape → execution)
- Command work (Hybrid B Mound Blend)
- Live ABs

### In-Season
- **Starters**: No Throw Recovery / Bullpen / Recovery / Hybrid B / Recovery+Short Box / Game Day / Recovery
- **Relievers**: Recovery+Short Box / Appearance / Recovery / Hybrid B / Recovery+Short Box / Appearance / Recovery
