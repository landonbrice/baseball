Gemmini Research Plan

### PART 1: FLEXOR-PRONATOR MASS (FPM) EXERCISES

The FPM serves as the primary dynamic stabilizer of the UCL. Total weekly volume for FPM should be 6-9 sets per movement type. Exercises should be performed **after** throwing and **after** lifting to avoid throwing while fatigued.

**DB Wrist Flexion Curl**

* **Muscles:** Flexor Carpi Ulnaris (FCU), Flexor Digitorum Superficialis (FDS)
* **Protocol:** 3 sets × 10-12 reps @ 10-22 lbs (start at ~5% BW)
* **Tempo:** 2:0:2 (controlled eccentric)
* **Frequency:** 2-3 days/week in-season
* **Rotation timing:** Day 1, Day 3, Day 5 / Avoid Day 0, Day 6
* **Contraindications:** Sharp medial elbow pain during movement
* **Source:** Lin & Anz 2025 Study

**DB Wrist Extension Curl**

* **Muscles:** Extensor Carpi Radialis Longus/Brevis
* **Protocol:** 3 sets × 10-12 reps @ 8-17 lbs (maintain 1:1.5 ratio with flexors)
* **Tempo:** 1:0:2
* **Frequency:** 2-3 days/week
* **Rotation timing:** Day 1, Day 3, Day 5
* **Contraindications:** Lateral epicondylitis symptoms
* **Source:** Lin & Anz 2025 Study

**Sledgehammer Pronation/Supination**

* **Muscles:** Pronator Teres (PT), Supinator
* **Protocol:** 3 sets × 10-15 reps (grip closer to head to regress, further to progress)
* **Tempo:** 3-second eccentric (lowering)
* **Frequency:** 2 days/week
* **Rotation timing:** Day 2, Day 4
* **Contraindications:** UCL insufficiency (active supination can increase stress if uncontrolled)
* **Source:** udall et al. EMG analysis

**Ulnar/Radial Deviation (Dumbbell or Hammer)**

* **Muscles:** FCU (Ulnar deviation), Brachioradialis (Radial deviation)
* **Protocol:** 3 sets × 10 reps @ 8-10 lbs
* **Tempo:** Controlled (2:0:2)
* **Frequency:** 2-3 days/week
* **Rotation timing:** Day 1, Day 3, Day 5
* **Contraindications:** Nerve symptoms in 4th/5th digit (ulnar nerve irritation)
* **Source:** Lin et al. EMG Study

**Finger Flexion / Grip Training (Flex Pro Grip or Heavy Crushers)**

* **Muscles:** Flexor Digitorum Superficialis (FDS), Flexor Digitorum Profundus (FDP)
* **Protocol:** 3 sets × 12 reps @ RPE 7-8
* **Tempo:** 1-second hold at peak contraction
* **Frequency:** 2 days/week
* **Rotation timing:** Day 2, Day 5
* **Contraindications:** Acute finger tendonitis
* **Source:** Saito et al. 2021

**BFR Forearm Protocol (Blood Flow Restriction)**

* **Muscles:** Total Forearm Mass
* **Protocol:** 5 sets (4 sets × 10 reps + 1 set to failure) @ 30-37% 1RM; 100-120 mmHg pressure
* **Tempo:** 1:0:1
* **Frequency:** 2 days/week
* **Rotation timing:** Day 1, Day 4 (High stress days)
* **Contraindications:** Vascular issues, history of blood clots
* **Source:** Casey & Bailey DIII Baseball Study

**Rice Bucket Work**

* **Muscles:** Forearm stabilizers, intrinsic hand muscles
* **Protocol:** 3 rounds × 60 seconds (mix of digs, circles, and grabs)
* **Tempo:** Continuous max-effort movement
* **Frequency:** 2-3 days/week
* **Rotation timing:** Day 3, Day 5 (Post-throwing)
* **Contraindications:** Open wounds or skin irritation
* **Source:** ASMI Thrower's Ten Supplementary

### PART 2: SHOULDER & SCAPULAR STABILITY EXERCISES

A healthy college pitcher should maintain an **ER:IR strength ratio of 67-75%**. A healthy **Total Arc of Motion (ER + IR @ 90°)** is approximately **172-175 degrees**.

**Rhythmic Stabilization (Ball on Wall)**

* **Muscles:** Rotator Cuff (Co-contraction), Scapular stabilizers
* **Protocol:** 3 sets × 30 seconds (partner-applied perturbations)
* **Tempo:** High-frequency small oscillations
* **Frequency:** 2-3 days/week
* **Rotation timing:** Day 1, Day 3, Day 6 (Activation)
* **Contraindications:** Acute labral tear
* **Source:** Mike Reinold

**Prone Y-T-W-A Raises**

* **Muscles:** Lower/Middle Trapezius, Rhomboids, Posterior Deltoid
* **Protocol:** 3 sets × 12-15 reps @ 2.5-5 lb DBs or BW
* **Tempo:** 2-second hold at top
* **Frequency:** 3 days/week
* **Rotation timing:** Day 2, Day 4, Day 6
* **Contraindications:** Impingement in the "Y" position
* **Source:** ASMI Advanced Thrower's Ten

**Bottoms-Up Kettlebell Press (1/2 Kneeling)**

* **Muscles:** Serratus Anterior, Rotator Cuff, Internal Oblique
* **Protocol:** 3 sets × 8 reps @ RPE 7
* **Tempo:** Slow and controlled stability focus
* **Frequency:** 2 days/week
* **Rotation timing:** Day 1, Day 4
* **Contraindications:** Shoulder instability
* **Source:** Cressey Sports Performance

**Cross-Body Stretch (Modified)**

* **Muscles:** Posterior Capsule
* **Protocol:** 3 sets × 30-second hold (stabilize scapula against wall)
* **Tempo:** Constant low-intensity tension
* **Frequency:** Daily
* **Rotation timing:** Day 0-6 (Daily maintenance)
* **Contraindications:** Anterior shoulder pain (stop immediately)
* **Source:** Wilk et al. Randomized Controlled Trial

### PART 3: LOWER BODY & ROTATIONAL POWER EXERCISES

Leg drive contributes 50-60% of pitching velocity.

**Trap Bar Deadlift (Strength Phase)**

* **Muscles:** Glutes, Hamstrings, Quads, Erector Spinae
* **Protocol:** 3 sets × 3-5 reps @ 85% 1RM
* **Tempo:** 1:1:1 (Controlled eccentric)
* **Frequency:** 1 day/week (Primary lift)
* **Rotation timing:** Day 1 (Post-outing)
* **Contraindications:** History of lumbar disk issues
* **Source:** TRACT Performance / Driveline

**Trap Bar Deadlift (Power/Speed Phase)**

* **Muscles:** Glutes, Hamstrings
* **Protocol:** 4 sets × 2 reps @ 50% 1RM (Max intent bar speed > 1.0 m/s)
* **Tempo:** Explosive concentric
* **Frequency:** 1 day/week
* **Rotation timing:** Day 4
* **Contraindications:** Movement quality breakdown at speed
* **Source:** JuggernautAI / Cressey

**Rotational Med Ball Throw (Shot Put Style)**

* **Ball Weight:** 4-6 lbs for college pitchers
* **Protocol:** 3 sets × 6-8 throws (Max Intent)
* **Frequency:** 2 days/week
* **Rotation timing:** Day 2, Day 4 (Paired with heavy lifting or sprints)
* **Contraindications:** Poor hip-shoulder separation mechanics
* **Source:** Premier Pitching Performance / Cressey

**Lateral Bounds (Heiden Jumps)**

* **Muscles:** Glute Medius, Adductors
* **Protocol:** 3 sets × 5 reps/side (Focus on distance and "sticking" the landing)
* **Frequency:** 2 days/week
* **Rotation timing:** Day 2, Day 4
* **Contraindications:** Knee ligament instability
* **Source:** Driveline Baseball

### PART 4: UPPER BODY PRESSING & PULLING EXERCISES

Recommended **Pull:Push ratio is 2:1** for overhead athletes. Horizontal pulls (rows) should be performed twice as often as vertical pulls (chin-ups).

**DB Bench Press (Neutral Grip)**

* **Muscles:** Pectoralis Major, Triceps, Anterior Deltoid
* **Protocol:** 3 sets × 8-10 reps @ RPE 8
* **Tempo:** 2:1:1
* **Frequency:** 1-2 days/week
* **Rotation timing:** Day 2, Day 4 / Avoid Day 6
* **Note:** Use 1-Arm variations to allow free scapular rotation.
* **Source:** Cressey Sports Performance

**1-Arm Landmine Press**

* **Muscles:** Anterior Deltoid, Serratus Anterior, Triceps
* **Protocol:** 3 sets × 8 reps @ RPE 7-8
* **Frequency:** 1-2 days/week
* **Rotation timing:** Day 1, Day 4
* **Note:** Superior substitute for OHP as it allows scapular upward rotation without impingement.
* **Source:** TRACT Performance

**Seal Row / Prone Row (DB or Barbell)**

* **Muscles:** Rhomboids, Middle Trapezius, Posterior Deltoid
* **Protocol:** 4 sets × 8-12 reps @ RPE 8
* **Frequency:** 2-3 days/week (Volume should double pushing sets)
* **Rotation timing:** Day 1, Day 2, Day 4
* **Source:** Eric Cressey

### PART 5: COMPLETE 7-DAY STARTER TEMPLATE

| Day | Intent | Main Lift Block | Accessory Block | Arm Care Block |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **0** | **Game** | High-intent outing | N/A | Post-game mobility |
| **1** | **Strength** | Trap Bar Deadlift: 3x5 (85%) | Landmine Press: 3x8; Seal Row: 4x10 | FPM: Flexion/Extension: 3x10 |
| **2** | **Speed** | 10-15yd Sprints: 6-8 reps | 1-Arm DB Bench: 3x10; Pallof Press: 3x10s | Scap series: Y-T-W: 3x12 |
| **3** | **Recovery** | ROM Circuits / Movement | 1-Leg RDL (Unloaded): 2x10 | Rhythmic Stabilization: 3x30s |
| **4** | **Power** | Trap Bar Speed: 4x2 (50%) | Med Ball Rotational: 3x6; Box Jump: 3x3 | FPM: BFR Forearm or Rice Bucket |
| **5** | **Mobility** | Movement Flow (15-20 min) | Lateral Lunge: 3x8; Deadbug: 3x10/side | Band ER/IR series |
| **6** | **Tune-up** | Light Sprints (50% intent) | Core: Bird-Dog / Ab Wheel: 2x10 | Pre-start RAMP protocol |

* **Reliever Modification:** Lift Mon/Thu regardless of outing. If reliever throws >2 innings, perform Day 1 Strength lift the following morning. If short outing, keep schedule.
* **Yellow Flag (Arm Feel < 5/10):** Reduce lifting intensity to RPE < 6; remove med ball throws and weighted ball work.
* **Red Flag (WHOOP/HRV < 33%):** Cut total training volume by 50%; remove all high-intent speed/power work; focus exclusively on mobility.

### PART 6: WHAT DOESN'T WORK — PRACTICES TO AVOID

* **Barbell Bench Press:** Pins scapula to the bench, disrupting scapulo-humeral rhythm.
* **Barbell Overhead Press (OHP):** High risk of subacromial impingement for overhead athletes with existing anterior instability.
* **Sleeper Stretch (Traditional):** Frequently causes subacromial impingement and irritates the anterior capsule. Use Modified Cross-Body Stretch instead.
* **Ice for Recovery:** Not supported for normal soreness; can delay tissue healing. Use movement/mobility instead.
* **High-Volume Leg Day on Day 1:** If the outing was high-stress, volume must be managed to allow CNS recovery, though intensity (load) can remain high.
