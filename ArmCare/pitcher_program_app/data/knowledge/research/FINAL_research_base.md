# UChicago Pitcher Training System — Synthesized Research Base
## Sources: Gemini Research, Tread Athletics In-Season Blueprint, Cressey Sports Performance, Driveline Baseball

---

## CRITICAL FRAMEWORK: WHERE THE EXPERTS AGREE

Before diving into exercises, here's what ALL four sources converge on. These are your non-negotiable system rules:

### Universal Principles (High Confidence)
1. **Heavy lower body work within 24 hours of a start** — Cressey is explicit: "I like heavy lower body sessions within 24 hours after a start." Tread and Driveline concur. The logic is "consolidation of stress" — stack the training stress near the outing so the pitcher has maximum recovery time before the next start.

2. **Push-ups > Barbell bench for pitchers** — Cressey: "If we do three days of horizontal pushing, two will be push-ups and one will be dumbbell pressing." Tread lists barbell bench as poor risk/reward. Gemini concurs. Push-ups allow free scapular movement; benching pins scapulae.

3. **Front squat > Back squat** — Cressey: "Use more front squatting, and little to no back squatting." Tread programs front squat and safety bar box squat as primary lower body movements. Back squat absent from all baseball-specific programs.

4. **Eliminate barbell overhead pressing** — Universal across all sources. Tread: "May be applicable in some cases, but carries a risk of impingement." Cressey lists it as contraindicated. Landmine press is the standard substitute.

5. **Pull:Push ratio of 2:1 minimum** — All sources agree. Tread programs double the pulling sets vs pushing. Cressey emphasizes horizontal and vertical pulling as primary upper body work.

6. **Maintain training frequency (3x/week lifting), reduce volume** — Tread: "Maintain frequency at 3-4 sessions/week, lower intensity to 85-90% of baseline, reduce total volume by 40-50%." Cressey: "In-season training is about frequency more than duration."

7. **No distance running** — Cressey wrote an entire article series debunking this. Sprint work and interval conditioning only. Pitchers rarely run more than 15 yards in a game.

8. **Mobility is as important as strength in-season** — Cressey: "Guys don't just get hurt in-season because they lose strength; they get hurt because they lose mobility."

9. **Avoid excessive eccentric emphasis in-season** — Tread: "The eccentric phase is largely responsible for DOMS 24-72 hours later. Doing frequent slow negatives is not advised in-season."

10. **You should NOT fail reps in-season** — Tread: "Use roughly 85-90% of what you might normally use for the specified rep range." The goal is maintenance and quality, not testing limits.


### Screening Benchmarks (from Wilk et al. prospective studies — GPT extraction)
These are implementable thresholds for intake assessment and ongoing monitoring:

| Metric | Threshold | Risk | Action |
|--------|-----------|------|--------|
| Total shoulder rotation deficit (throwing vs non-throwing) | >5° deficit | ~2.6× injury risk | Flag for mobility emphasis, add daily cross-body stretch + sleeper stretch alternative, recheck in 2 weeks |
| Shoulder flexion deficit (throwing side) | ≥5° deficit | ~2.8× injury risk | Flag for lat/posterior shoulder mobility work, reduce heavy pulling volume |
| Supraspinatus weakness (preseason) | Below baseline | Elevated upper extremity injury risk | Increase scapular stability and ER work frequency |
| ER:IR strength ratio | <67% | Increased shoulder injury risk | Add ER strengthening, reduce pressing volume |
| Total arc of motion (ER + IR @ 90°) | <172° | Below normative range | Daily ROM work, flag if declining over season |

### ROM Recovery Time-Course (constrains where heavy work goes)
- ROM decreases are measurable immediately post-pitching and persist at 24 hours (Reinold et al.)
- Glenohumeral internal rotation can remain below baseline at 72 hours (Kibler et al.)
- **Bot rule:** No high-eccentric, high-volume upper body work within 24 hours of a start. Heavy LOWER body is fine (and recommended) within 24h. Heavy upper body waits until Day 2 minimum.

### FPM Training — Honest Framing for Bot Communication
Lopez et al. found that an 8-week forearm strengthening program in college pitchers increased grip strength and forearm girth, but static medial elbow gapping under valgus load was statistically unchanged. This means:
- **Bot should frame FPM work as:** "Building fatigue resistance and capacity in the muscles that protect your UCL during high-pitch-count outings"
- **Bot should NOT frame FPM work as:** "Strengthening your UCL" or "preventing UCL tears"
- The value is durability and fatigue resistance across a long season, not structural ligament change

### Bot Decision Logic (enhanced triage flowchart)
```
1. Pain > 2/10 or new swelling? → YES → Medical eval / deload + modify throwing. STOP.
2. ROM red flags hit? (>5° total rotation deficit, ≥5° flexion deficit) → YES → Mobility + low-load cuff/scap, avoid heavy pressing
3. Grip/finger flexion drop vs baseline? → YES → Reduce forearm load today, emphasize recovery + capacity work later in week
4. Start within 48 hours? → YES → Primer session only (low volume, high intent, no new exercises)
5. None of the above → Full strength/power session per template
```

### Where the Experts DISAGREE (Bot Should Know)

| Topic | Cressey | Tread | Driveline | Gemini/Research |
|-------|---------|-------|-----------|----------------|
| **Med ball work in-season** | Minimal — "overwhelming amount of accidental power training already happening" | Not addressed in detail | Integrated with plyo ball routines | Recommends 2x/week |
| **FPM training volume in-season** | Part of overall cuff work, 2x/week | "Avoid excessive forearm training — these muscles are constantly fighting to recover" | Not addressed directly | 6-9 sets/week, 2-3x |
| **Day 1 post-outing intent** | "Challenging lower body lift" (high intensity) | Lower Body A (strength focus) | Varies by program | "Consolidate stress" — agrees with heavy |
| **Rotator cuff frequency** | 2x/week conventional cuff + 2x/week rhythmic stab | Integrated into upper body days | Part of arm care protocols | 2-3x/week |
| **Chin-ups/Pull-ups for pitchers** | Strongly pro: "Far too many coaches have tried to beat that dead horse; let it go" | Programs them as primary vertical pull | Standard inclusion | Supports |

**Bot default when experts disagree:** Follow Cressey model for in-season management (most established with baseball-specific population), supplement with Gemini FPM protocols for UCL protection (our specific priority given Lando's history).

**On the FPM debate specifically:** Tread says avoid excessive forearm work in-season because these muscles are already fatigued from throwing. Gemini/research says 6-9 sets/week. The resolution: LOW volume, HIGH frequency FPM work (2-3 sets × 10-15 reps, light load, 3-4 days/week) rather than high-volume forearm sessions. This provides the protective stimulus without digging a recovery hole. For pitchers with UCL history, lean toward the higher end of this range.

## COMPLETE EXERCISE DATABASE

### CATEGORY 1: PRIMARY LOWER BODY COMPOUNDS

**1. Trap Bar Deadlift**
- Muscles: Glutes, hamstrings, quads, erector spinae
- STRENGTH: 3×3-5 @ 85% 1RM | Rest: 3 min
- POWER/SPEED: 4×2 @ 50% 1RM, max bar speed intent (>1.0 m/s) | Rest: 2-3 min
- Rotation: Strength on Day 1, Speed on Day 4 | Avoid Day 0, 5, 6
- Source: Tread (Lower B primary), Driveline, Gemini
- Contraindications: Lumbar disk issues → substitute belt squat
- Note: Tread in-season rule applies — use 85-90% of normal training weight

**2. Front Squat**
- Muscles: Quads, glutes, anterior core (bracing demand)
- Protocol: 3×4-6 @ 75-85% 1RM | Rest: 3 min
- Rotation: Day 1 or Day 4 (alternate with trap bar DL as primary) | Avoid Day 5, 6
- Source: Cressey (primary lower body movement for baseball), Tread (Lower A primary)
- Contraindications: Wrist/elbow pain in rack position → use safety bar box squat or belt squat
- Note: Cressey explicitly prefers front squat over back squat for baseball

**3. Barbell Hip Thrust**
- Muscles: Glute max (horizontal force production)
- Protocol: 3×8-10 @ 70-80% 1RM | Rest: 2 min
- Rotation: Day 1 or Day 4
- Source: Tread (Lower B option), Contreras research
- Contraindications: Low back pain with bridging → single leg variation

**4. Rear-Foot-Elevated Split Squat (Bulgarian)**
- Muscles: Quads, glutes, hip stabilizers (single-leg)
- Protocol: 3×6-8/side @ RPE 7-8 (DBs or front-rack barbell) | Rest: 90s
- Rotation: Day 2 or Day 4
- Source: Tread (Lower B unilateral slot), Cressey (single-leg work on Day 3)
- Contraindications: Knee ligament instability → reverse lunge substitute
- Note: Directly mimics single-leg force production of pitching delivery

**5. Romanian Deadlift (Bilateral)**
- Muscles: Hamstrings (eccentric), glutes, erector spinae
- Protocol: 3×8-10 @ 65-75% 1RM | Rest: 2 min
- Rotation: Day 1 or Day 4 (posterior chain slot)
- Source: Tread (Lower A posterior chain), universal programming
- Contraindications: Low back history → single-leg RDL at lighter load

**6. Single-Leg RDL (Loaded)**
- Muscles: Hamstrings, glute med (stability), posterior chain
- Protocol: 3×8/side @ moderate DB | Rest: 60-90s
- Rotation: Day 2 or Day 4 | Unloaded version acceptable on Day 3
- Source: Tread (Lower B posterior chain), Cressey
- Contraindications: Balance issues → hand on wall for support


### CATEGORY 2: PLYOMETRICS & ROTATIONAL POWER

**7. Rotational Med Ball Throw (Shot Put Style)**
- Muscles: Obliques, hip rotators, trunk rotation power
- Protocol: 3×6-8 throws/side, MAX INTENT, 4-6 lb ball | Rest: 60-90s between sets
- Rotation: Day 2, Day 4 | Avoid Day 0, 5, 6
- Source: Driveline, Cressey (pre-lifting), Gemini
- Note: Cressey reduces med ball volume significantly in-season. Use 2x/week max. Perform BEFORE lifting, not after.

**8. Med Ball Scoop Toss**
- Muscles: Hip extensors, obliques, anterior core (low-to-high pattern)
- Protocol: 3×5-6 throws/side, max intent, 4-6 lb ball
- Rotation: Day 2 or Day 4
- Source: Driveline, Premier Pitching Performance

**9. Med Ball Overhead Slam**
- Muscles: Lats, anterior core, deceleration training
- Protocol: 3×6-8 slams, 6-10 lb ball
- Rotation: Day 2 or Day 4
- Source: Cressey Sports Performance

**10. Lateral Bounds (Heiden Jumps)**
- Muscles: Glute medius, adductors, lateral power
- Protocol: 3×5/side, focus on distance + "sticking" the landing | Rest: 90s
- Rotation: Day 2, Day 4
- Source: Driveline, Gemini
- Contraindications: Knee ligament instability

**11. Broad Jump**
- Muscles: Glutes, quads (triple extension power)
- Protocol: 4×3, max intent, full reset between reps | Rest: 90s
- Rotation: Day 2 or Day 4
- Source: Standard plyometric programming

**12. Nordic Hamstring Curl**
- Muscles: Hamstrings (eccentric emphasis — deceleration)
- Protocol: 3×3-5 (build slowly; in-season keep volume low due to eccentric load)
- Rotation: Day 4 only (furthest from outing) | Avoid in-season if causing excessive DOMS
- Source: van der Horst injury prevention meta-analyses
- Note: Tread warns against excessive eccentrics in-season. Use sparingly, monitor soreness.


### CATEGORY 3: UPPER BODY PULLING

**13. Weighted Chin-Up / Pull-Up**
- Muscles: Lats, biceps, grip, scapular depression
- Protocol: 3×5-6 @ BW+ (add load when BW×8+ is easy) | Rest: 2-3 min
- Rotation: Day 2 (upper body emphasis day)
- Source: Cressey (strongly endorses for pitchers)
- Contraindications: Shoulder impingement in overhead position → neutral grip or substitute rows

**14. Chest-Supported / Seal Row (DB or Barbell)**
- Muscles: Rhomboids, mid-traps, posterior deltoid, lats
- Protocol: 3-4×8-12 @ RPE 8 | Rest: 90s
- Rotation: Day 1, Day 2, Day 4 (primary horizontal pull — high frequency)
- Source: Tread (Upper A primary pull), Cressey, Gemini
- Note: This is the workhorse pulling exercise. Volume should roughly double pushing.

**15. Half-Kneeling Single-Arm Cable Row**
- Muscles: Lats, rhomboids, rotary stability (anti-rotation demand)
- Protocol: 3×10-12/side @ moderate | Rest: 60-90s
- Rotation: Day 2, Day 4
- Source: Tread (Upper B pull option), Cressey Sports Performance

**16. Face Pull (Band or Cable)**
- Muscles: Rear deltoid, mid-traps, external rotators
- Protocol: 2-3×15-20 | Rest: 60s
- Rotation: Day 1-5 (can be performed almost daily as activation)
- Source: Tread (Upper A pull accessory), universal arm health programming


### CATEGORY 4: UPPER BODY PUSHING

**17. Push-Up Variations (Standard, Deficit, Banded)**
- Muscles: Pecs, anterior deltoid, triceps, serratus anterior (protraction at top)
- Protocol: 3×10-15 (bodyweight) or 3×8-10 (banded/deficit) | Rest: 60-90s
- Rotation: Day 1 (light), Day 2 (moderate), Day 4 (moderate)
- Source: Cressey (primary in-season pressing movement), Tread
- Note: Cressey: "If we do three days of horizontal pushing, two will be push-ups." Allows free scapular movement.

**18. Neutral-Grip DB Bench Press**
- Muscles: Pecs, triceps, anterior deltoid
- Protocol: 3×8-10 @ RPE 7-8 | Rest: 2 min
- Rotation: Day 2 or Day 4 (1x/week max) | Avoid Day 5, 6
- Source: Tread (Upper A main push), Cressey (1x/week in-season)
- Note: Use 1-arm variations to allow scapular rotation. Neutral grip reduces anterior shoulder stress.

**19. Half-Kneeling Landmine Press (Single-Arm)**
- Muscles: Anterior deltoid, serratus anterior, triceps
- Protocol: 3×8/side @ RPE 7-8 | Rest: 90s
- Rotation: Day 1 or Day 4
- Source: Tread (push accessory), Gemini
- Note: THE substitute for overhead pressing. Allows scapular upward rotation without impingement risk.

**20. Push-Up Plus**
- Muscles: Serratus anterior (protraction emphasis at end-range)
- Protocol: 3×12-15 | Can be daily as activation
- Rotation: Day 1, 3, 5 (warm-up or arm care circuit)
- Source: Reinold, ASMI Thrower's Ten


### CATEGORY 5: SCAPULAR STABILITY & SHOULDER HEALTH

**21. Prone Y-T-W-A Raises**
- Muscles: Lower/mid-traps, rhomboids, posterior deltoid
- Protocol: 3×12-15 @ 2.5-5 lb DBs, 2-second hold at top | Rest: 60s
- Rotation: Day 2, 4, 6 (3x/week)
- Source: ASMI Advanced Thrower's Ten, Gemini

**22. Rhythmic Stabilization (Ball on Wall)**
- Muscles: Rotator cuff co-contraction, scapular stabilizers
- Protocol: 3×30 seconds (partner or self-applied perturbations at 90° abduction)
- Rotation: Cressey does 2x/week; Gemini suggests Day 1, 3, 6
- Source: Reinold, Cressey

**23. Bottoms-Up Kettlebell Press (Half-Kneeling)**
- Muscles: Rotator cuff, serratus anterior, internal oblique
- Protocol: 3×8/side @ RPE 7 (slow, controlled) | Rest: 60s
- Rotation: Day 1, Day 4
- Source: Cressey Sports Performance, Gemini

**24. Band External Rotation at 90° Abduction**
- Muscles: Infraspinatus, teres minor
- Protocol: 2-3×12-15/side | Light resistance
- Rotation: 2x/week (Cressey recommendation for conventional cuff work)
- Source: Reinold, ASMI Thrower's Ten, Cressey

**25. Band Internal Rotation**
- Muscles: Subscapularis
- Protocol: 2×12-15/side | Light resistance
- Rotation: Paired with ER work
- Source: ASMI Thrower's Ten

**26. Cross-Body Stretch (Modified)**
- Muscles: Posterior capsule
- Protocol: 3×30-second hold (stabilize scapula against wall) | Daily
- Source: Wilk et al. RCT — superior to sleeper stretch
- Note: Sleeper stretch is NOT recommended (Gemini, Cressey, Tread all flag impingement risk)

**27. Band Pull-Apart**
- Muscles: Rear deltoid, mid-traps
- Protocol: 3×15-20 | Can be daily warm-up
- Rotation: Every training day
- Source: Universal arm health, Jaeger


### CATEGORY 6: FLEXOR-PRONATOR MASS (UCL PROTECTION)

**28. DB Wrist Flexion Curl**
- Muscles: FCU, FDS (primary UCL dynamic stabilizers)
- Protocol: 2-3×10-15 @ 10-22 lbs (start ~5% BW) | Tempo: 2:0:2
- Rotation: Day 1, 3, 5 (3x/week) | Avoid Day 0, 6
- Source: Lin & Anz 2025
- Note: Perform AFTER throwing and AFTER lifting. Keep volume low per Tread warning.

**29. DB Wrist Extension Curl**
- Muscles: Extensor carpi radialis longus/brevis
- Protocol: 2-3×10-12 @ 8-17 lbs (maintain 1:1.5 ratio with flexors) | Tempo: 1:0:2
- Rotation: Paired with wrist flexion
- Source: Lin & Anz 2025

**30. Sledgehammer Pronation/Supination**
- Muscles: Pronator teres, supinator
- Protocol: 2-3×10-15 (grip closer to head to regress, further to progress) | 3-sec eccentric
- Rotation: Day 2, Day 4 (2x/week)
- Source: Udall et al. EMG analysis, Gemini

**31. Finger Flexion / Grip Training**
- Muscles: FDS, FDP
- Protocol: 3×12 @ RPE 7-8 | 1-sec hold at peak contraction
- Rotation: Day 2, Day 5 (2x/week)
- Source: Saito et al. 2021

**32. Rice Bucket Work**
- Muscles: Forearm stabilizers, intrinsic hand muscles
- Protocol: 3 rounds × 60 seconds (digs, circles, grabs) | Continuous
- Rotation: Day 3, Day 5 (post-throwing)
- Source: ASMI supplementary

**33. BFR Forearm Protocol**
- Muscles: Total forearm mass
- Protocol: 4×10 + 1 set to failure @ 30-37% 1RM | 100-120 mmHg
- Rotation: Day 1, Day 4 (2x/week) — on higher stress training days
- Source: Casey & Bailey DIII study
- Contraindications: Vascular issues, blood clots


### CATEGORY 7: CORE / ANTI-ROTATION

**34. Pallof Press**
- Muscles: Internal oblique, transverse abdominis (anti-rotation)
- Protocol: 3×8-10/side, 3-second hold at extension | Rest: 60s
- Rotation: Day 2, Day 4
- Source: Standard anti-rotation, widely used in baseball S&C

**35. Dead Bug**
- Muscles: Anterior core, hip flexors (anti-extension)
- Protocol: 3×8-10/side (opposite arm/leg) | Slow, controlled
- Rotation: Daily warm-up movement (Day 1-5)
- Source: DNS-based core training

**36. Ab Wheel Rollout**
- Muscles: Anterior core, lats (anti-extension under load)
- Protocol: 3×8-10 from knees | Rest: 60s
- Rotation: Day 2 or Day 4
- Source: McGill core stability research

**37. Side Plank Variations**
- Muscles: Lateral core, hip stabilizers
- Protocol: 2-3×20-30 seconds/side
- Rotation: Day 1-4 (can be incorporated frequently)
- Source: Tread (Lower A lateral core), McGill


### CATEGORY 8: CONDITIONING & SPEED

**38. Short Sprints (10-15 yard starts)**
- Protocol: 6-8 reps from various starting positions | Full recovery between
- Rotation: Day 2 (movement training day per Cressey)
- Source: Cressey ("Day 2: movement training focused on 10-15yd starts, agility, and some top speed")

**39. Sled Push/Drag**
- Protocol: 4-6 pushes × 20-30 yards OR 8×8-second max effort pushes (rest 52s)
- Rotation: Day 1 or Day 3 (low eccentric load = minimal soreness)
- Source: Tread (Conditioning B option)
- Note: Sled work is recovery-friendly — no eccentric component means no DOMS

**40. Moderate-Intensity Intervals (Conditioning A)**
- Protocol: 12-16 rounds of 20s on / 20s off @ 70% effort (bike, tempo runs, slideboard)
- Rotation: Day 0 (post-game) or Day 3
- Source: Tread Athletics
- Note: This is the "cardio" component. Keep it low-intensity and non-invasive.

### Reliever Modification
- Lift Mon/Thu regardless of outing schedule
- If reliever throws >2 innings: perform Day 1 strength lift next morning, then Day 2 lift 48 hours later
- If short outing (<20 pitches): Cressey model — go right to Short Option 1 (single-leg + upper emphasis, 8-12 sets), then Short Option 2 (lower emphasis, 8-12 sets), then day off
- Keep total weekly volume at 2-3 lifting sessions, full-body, moderate volume

### Flag Modifications
- **GREEN (Arm feel ≥4, WHOOP ≥66%):** Full program as written
- **YELLOW (Arm feel 3, WHOOP 33-66%):** Reduce all loads to RPE 6-7, remove med ball throws, maintain FPM volume, maintain compound movements but at reduced intensity
- **RED (Arm feel ≤2 OR WHOOP <33%):** Mobility/recovery only. No lifting. No high-intent throwing. If arm feel ≤2 for 2+ consecutive days → flag for trainer evaluation
- **DELOAD WEEK (every 4th week):** Reduce all loads 15-20%, reduce volume 30%. Maintain FPM and arm care volume (these don't deload).

### Progression Rules
- **Compounds (Trap bar, front squat, hip thrust):** All reps completed cleanly for 2 consecutive sessions → +5-10 lbs
- **Accessories (Split squat, RDL, rows):** All reps at RPE for 2 sessions → +2.5-5 lbs
- **FPM work:** Add 1 set or 2-3 reps before increasing load. Load increases in 1-2 lb increments.
- **Plyometrics/Med ball:** Never increase load. Progress by increasing intent or +1-2 throws per set.
