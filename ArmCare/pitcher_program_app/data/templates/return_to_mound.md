# Return to Mound Progression (~8 Weeks)
# Source: Return to mound progression - approx 8 weeks (4).xlsx
# Classification: Template for post-injury return to mound work
# Prerequisite: Completed Phase 1 (flat ground ramp up) with appropriate build-up

---

## Overview
Progressive return-to-mound program following completion of a flat-ground throwing ramp-up.
Introduces mound work, compression throws, pulldowns, and bullpens over ~8 weeks.

## Drill Progression (same as ramp-up)
- **45ft (50%):** High/pec load, snap-snap rocker, self toss
- **60ft (60%):** Figure 8 rocker, half kneel start, in the hole, step back
- **75ft (70%):** QB drop back, lateral bound
- **90ft (75%):** Standard long toss
- **105ft (80%):** Extended distance
- **120ft (85%):** Max extension

## Weekly Progression

### Week 1 — Establish Base + Compression
- Days 1 & 3: Full progression to 105ft, work back in with 80% on-a-line at 90/75ft
- Day 2: Lighter session to 90ft only
- 3 throwing days

### Week 2 — Introduce Short Box Bullpen
- Days 1 & 3: Full progression to 120ft with 80-85% compression
- Day 2: Add 15 throws on the mound at 50ft (groove fastball only)
- Compression throws increase to 85-90% on-a-line

### Week 3 — Add Pulldowns + Mound Work
- Day 1: Full progression + pulldowns at 105/90ft (100% intent) + 20 throws on bump at 50ft
- Day 2: Standard progression to 105ft
- Day 3: Standard to 90ft (lighter day)
- Day 4: Full progression with pulldowns at 105/90ft
- Increases to 4 throwing days

### Weeks 4-8 (continued progression)
- Gradually increase mound work distance (50ft → full 60.5ft)
- Increase mound volume (15 → 20 → 25+ pitches)
- Introduce off-speed pitches after fastball command established
- Progress from short box to full bullpens
- Eventually introduce live ABs

## Key Safety Principles
- Must complete Phase 1 flat-ground ramp-up first
- Monitor volume (total throws) and intensity (% effort) closely
- Compression/pulldown throws are highest stress — introduce gradually
- Mound work starts at 50ft, not full distance
- Fastball only until comfortable, then add secondaries
- 4 throwing days per week max at peak
